Our History

Founded in 2002, the Institut Universitaire des Grandes Ecoles des Tropiques (IUGET) emerged as a response to Cameroonâ€™s growing demand for quality higher education aligned with international standards and local development needs.
Established in Douala, the economic capital of Cameroon, IUGET was born out of a vision to bridge the gap between academic theory and practical industry skills. From its inception, IUGET placed a strong emphasis on technology, engineering, business,
and applied sciences, quickly gaining recognition as one of the leading private universities in the region. Its mission: to equip students with the tools necessary to thrive in a competitive global workforce while contributing meaningfully
to national progress. Over the years, IUGET has grown from a modest academic institution to a comprehensive university with several faculties, modern laboratories, international partnerships,
and a diverse student body. With a commitment to innovation, excellence, and entrepreneurship, the university continues to play a pivotal role in shaping the next generation
of leaders in Cameroon and beyond. Today, IUGET stands as a proud symbol of academic resilience, producing graduates who are both intellectually prepared and socially responsible.

A word from the Principal

The option taken by Cameroon to reach the emergence at the horizon 2035, finds in this context a resonance bringing great hopes in terms of employment and career opportunities.
We are convinced that, despite the unemployment rate that strongly affects the young population, the future remains promising if they receive adequate training.
